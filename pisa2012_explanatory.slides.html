How Gender and Immigrant Background Affect Student Standing¶

by Marc Angelo Acebedo¶

Investigation Overview¶

For the PISA 2012 dataset, I wanted to see how environment and background variables factor into student drive and academic performance.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consists of around 460,000 students from 65 countries. I isolate my focus on the above listed 4 attributes, and the resulting dataset went down from over 600 columns to only 14. Many columns were dropped due to irrelevance in my investigation purposes. Here is a breakdown of each variable group:

CAUSES¶

  • Environment: Teacher helpfulness, sense of belonging, acculturation
  • Background: Country, gender, parent occupational background, mother immigrant background

EFFECTS¶

  • Student Drive: Work ethic, future optimism, self esteem
  • Academic Performance: Math, Reading, and Science Scores

Though my focus could have taken many different paths, I chose the following two approaches for my key insights.

Approach 1. Immigrant Background by Drive and Academic Performance¶

Distribution of Students with Immigrant Mothers¶

Of all the students who answered the Mother Immigrant question, the overwhelming majority of students answered "Yes" at 77.83%. The difference is so staggering that the difference in proportions is 55.66% in favor of students with immigrant backgrounds.

Immigrant Background by Academic Performance (Scores)¶

There is a vast disparity in records for students whose mothers aren't or are immigrants. Therefore, we cannot rely on the following histograms for sole counts.

As it was previously established, students from immigrant backgrounds score lower for work ethic, future optimism, and self-esteem. However, they score consistently higher than students not from an immigrant background.

This is a key takeaway: students with immigrant mothers tend to have a lower student drive and a more pessimistic views of themselves, but consistently outperform non-immigrant students by a large margin.

Approach 2. Gender by Student Drive and Academic Performance¶

Distribution of Male and Female Students¶

Gender is about evenly split with female students at 50.5% and males at 49.5%, so we can safely say that conclusions drawn by gender are fair and representative.

Gender by Student Drive Features¶

Girls tend to have a higher work ethic but significantly less self-esteem than boys. Meanwhile, there is no gender gap for future optimism.

This is a key takeaway as this discovery reflects the reality that girls are often discouraged and intimidated in academic settings, despite putting more in more effort on average.

Is this outperformance in reading scores due to girls' higher work ethic? What about the other scores? Let's look deeper.¶

Gender by Academic Performance and Work Ethic¶

Despite girls having an overall higher work ethic, if a boy and girl had the same work ethic score, the boy is most certainly guaranteed to score higher for math. However, the contrary is true for reading scores.¶
